# 🚗 Vehicle Market Share Analysis (U.S. States)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project is part of an Analyst Builder data analytics exercise. It focuses on analyzing vehicle fuel type distribution across U.S. states, with emphasis on Electric Vehicles (EVs), Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs), Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs), and gasoline vehicles.

The objective is to understand EV adoption trends, compare states, and identify opportunities for EV infrastructure development.

---

## ⚙️ Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel  
- Pivot Tables  
- Slicers  
- Charts & Dashboard Design  

---

## 📊 Dataset Description
The dataset contains state-wise vehicle registrations across different fuel types:
- Electric Vehicles (EV)  
- Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEV)  
- Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEV)  
- Gasoline Vehicles  
- Other fuels like Biodiesel, Ethanol (E85), CNG, Hydrogen, etc.

---

## 🧹 Data Preparation
- Cleaned and structured raw dataset  
- Standardized fuel type categories  
- Built pivot tables for analysis  
- Calculated percentage distribution of fuel types per state  
- Created an interactive dashboard using slicers  

---

## 📈 Analysis Performed

### ✔ Market Share Analysis
- Calculated distribution of EV, PHEV, HEV, and gasoline vehicles  
- Compared fuel adoption across all U.S. states  

### ✔ EV Adoption Analysis
- Identified top states for EV adoption  
- Compared major states such as California, Texas, Florida, and Washington  

### ✔ Alternative Fuel Analysis
- Analyzed usage of biodiesel, ethanol, hydrogen, and CNG  
- Identified which fuels are widely used vs niche  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights
- Gasoline vehicles dominate across all states (>90%)  
- EV adoption is increasing but still relatively low  
- California and Washington lead in EV adoption  
- Most states are still in early stages of EV transition  
- Hydrogen and CNG usage remains minimal  

---

## 📌 Recommendations

### 🔹 Texas
Large vehicle population but low EV share → high potential for EV infrastructure investment  

### 🔹 Florida
High dependence on gasoline → requires stronger EV charging infrastructure  

### 🔹 Washington
Already high EV adoption → needs infrastructure expansion to support continued growth  

---

## 🎯 Conclusion
This analysis shows that while EV adoption is growing in the United States, gasoline vehicles still dominate the market. A few states are leading the transition, but most are still in early adoption stages, highlighting strong opportunities for EV infrastructure development.
